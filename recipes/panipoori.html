Pani Poori

Pani Puri, also known as Golgappa or Phuchka, is a popular Indian street food that consists of crispy, hollow puris (bread) filled with a flavorful mixture of spiced potatoes, onions, chickpeas, and tamarind water.

Ingredients

  • Sooji (Semolina)
  • Atta (Whole Wheat flour)
  • Water
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  1. Mix dry ingredients: Combine 1 cup fine sooji (semolina), 2 tablespoons atta (whole wheat flour), and a pinch of salt in a bowl.
  2. Add water: Gradually add ¼ cup hot water to the mixture and knead until a stiff dough forms. Knead for 5-6 minutes.
  3. Rest the dough: Cover the dough with a wet cloth and let it rest for 20-25 minutes.
  4. Knead again: Knead the dough for 1-2 minutes once it’s rested.
  5. Portion the dough: Divide the dough into 4 equal parts and roll out one part into a thin rope.
  6. Cut and shape: Cut the rope into small pieces (about the size of a blueberry) and roll each piece into a ball. Flatten each ball slightly into a disk shape.
  7. Roll out into circles: Use a rolling pin to roll out each disk into a circle, about 1 1/2 to 2 inches in diameter.
